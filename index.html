About Dr. Cook

After completing undergraduate and master's degrees in anthropology and history at McMaster University (Hamilton, Canada), Katherine pursued a Ph.D. in Archaeology at the University of York (UK), focussing on colonial cemeteries in Barbados in relation to memory, ethnicity and family.

Katherine was also part of the Institute on Digital Archaeology & Practice at Michigan State University and now focusses heavily on the interactions of heritage and digital technology, both in research and teaching.

She is a passionate advocate for ethical and responsible practice, collaborating with museums and heritage organisations for public engagement and education.
